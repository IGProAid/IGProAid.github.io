PURPOSIVE COMMUNICATION

Communication - a two-way process of giving and receiving information through any number of channel.


Functions of Communication:

- meet needs

- fulfill social obligation

- enhance or maintain sense of self

- develop relationship

- exchange information

- influence others


Culture - complex collection of knowledge, folklore, language, rules, roles, habits, lifestyles, attitudes, beliefs and custom.

Six Dimension of Culture

1. Distance Index

2. Individualism VS Collectivism

3. Masculinity VS Femininity

4. Uncertainty Avoidance Index

5. Long Term Orientation VS Short Term Normative Orientation

6. Indulgence VS. Restraint


Intercultural Communication and Competence - ability to communicate effectively

Characteristics of ICC:

- Self-Awareness

- Adaptability

- Empathy

- Cognitive Complexity

- Ethics

- Co-culture

- Perceptual Filters

Barriers to ICC

1. High Anxiety

2. Non-Verbal Misinterpretation

3. Ethnocentrism

4. Language

5. Assuming Similarity instead of Differences

6. Stereotypes and Prejudices
